# 7.3a Acceptance: Kiln Global Routing (`global_route`)

[< Back to README.md](../../README.md)

Measured acceptance run of Phase 7.3a's global (coarse) router,
`global_route(project_path, nets=None, connections=None)` in `kicad_router_tool.py`,
against the real kiln project (`kiln.kicad_pro` / `kiln.kicad_pcb`). All numbers below
are from actual instrumented runs against the checked-in board on 2026-07-22 (venv
`mykicadMcp\.venv\Scripts\python.exe`, project Python 3.14.6), using stock
`pcb_settings.json` defaults (the project has no `pcb_settings.json` override file, so
every weight below is `DEFAULT_PCB_SETTINGS`). This is a read-only measurement
document; no board or project file was modified to produce it.

**Result: 39/39 connections routed, 0 failed, 4 near-tie, correct bundle grouping
(4 bundles: 1 I2C + 3 SPI, all under `/MainControler/`).**

---

## 1. Runtimes

Measured with `time.perf_counter()` wrapped directly around two sequential
`global_route(PROJECT)` calls in the **same Python process**:

| Run | Wall clock | Cache state |
|---|---|---|
| Cold | **137.53 s** | First call in a fresh process: `kicad_pcb_tool`'s mtime/size parse caches (footprints, tracks, board layers, zone fills) are empty, so this includes both parsing and the coarse A* search. |
| Warm | **128.35 s** | Second call, same process, same board file untouched: parse caches are hot (mtime/size unchanged), so this isolates the A* search cost from parsing/file I/O. |

Cold vs. warm improvement is only **~6.7%** (9.2 s) — far less than "much faster."
**Finding that corrects the task's prior assumption:** the router has no result cache
of its own; every `global_route` call re-runs the full per-connection A* search from
scratch regardless of parse-cache state. Parsing/rasterizing the board (footprints,
1,609 segments, 298 vias, zone fills) is cheap relative to the search — the ~128 s
floor is A* expansion cost, not file I/O. This confirms and sharpens the prior
diagnosis (NETCLASS_PLAN note (b)): foreign-plane cells cost `_FULL_CELL_MILLI`
(5,000,000 milli) per step against an octile heuristic pricing a step at ~1,000
milli, so A* keeps expanding far past where a real router would prune — see §6 for
the exact magnitude (99.4% of total reported cost is `_FULL_CELL_MILLI`).

Both runs produced **byte-identical JSON** (`json.dumps(sort_keys=True)` equality on
the full result dict) — determinism holds.

## 4. Home layers

Home-layer distribution (`_dominant_layer` choice per connection):

| Net kind | F.Cu | B.Cu | In1.Cu | In2.Cu | Total |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| signal (20) | 14 | 6 | 0 | 0 | 20 |
| power (19) | 5 | 12 | 1 | 1 | 19 |

**Signal nets: matches intuition exactly.** All 20 signal connections home to a
signal-type layer (F.Cu or B.Cu); zero home to either power plane. This is exactly
what `layer_purpose.signal` (`{signal:1.0, power:4.0}`) predicts — a 4x per-step
penalty for putting a signal trace on a plane layer makes it essentially never win
against a same-cost F.Cu/B.Cu alternative.

**Power nets: partially counter-intuitive, and worth flagging.** Only 2 of 19 power
connections (GND_Main's longest island-pair at 7.14 mm, and one 3.3V_Main pair at
2.25 mm) home to their purpose-matched plane layer (In1.Cu/In2.Cu); the other 17 —
including every power connection under ~5 mm airline — home to F.Cu/B.Cu despite
`layer_purpose.power` giving planes a 2x cost *advantage* over signal-type layers
(`{power:1.0, signal:2.0}`). The explanation is the via cost, not a bug: reaching a
buried plane layer costs a via (`weights.via=25.0` -> `q()`=25,000 milli) plus the
target cell's congestion charge, and for a 1–3 mm airline that fixed via cost
dwarfs the 2x-vs-1x purpose-multiplier saving over the connection's short length —
so staying on the start pad's own signal layer (no via) wins on pure cost. Only the
two longest power connections (7.14 mm, 2.25 mm — still short in absolute terms)
tip the balance toward paying the via. **This is a real, quantified finding for the
coordinator to weigh**: whether short power hops should be biased more strongly
toward the plane (e.g. a lower `via` weight for power nets, or a `home_layer` prior
independent of the coarse-cost-only first pass) is a 7.5.4/plane-engine-adjacent
design question, not a defect in 7.3a's arithmetic — the formula is doing exactly
what its weights say.

## 6. Cost composition

`summary.total_est_cost_milli = 628,753,921` (the sum of each of the 39 reported
connections' winning-candidate `est_cost_milli`, **exactly as summed by
`global_route`, including the bundle-reuse duplication** described next).

### 6a. `_FULL_CELL_MILLI` share

Re-deriving each winning path's cost with a term-by-term breakdown (same arithmetic
as `_path_cost_milli`, instrumented to separate the `_FULL_CELL_MILLI` congestion
term from everything else — step/via base cost, direction-change, away-from-home,
off-corridor, and the non-full congestion charge `q(congestion/remaining)`),
re-scored against the exact model state each connection saw at commit time:

| Term | milli | Share of total |
|---|---|---|
| `_FULL_CELL_MILLI` congestion (125 full-cell steps x 5,000,000) | **625,000,000** | **99.40%** |
| Everything else (step, via, direction-change, away-from-home, off-corridor, partial congestion) | 3,753,921 | 0.60% |
| **Total** | **628,753,921** | 100% |

**The kiln total is essentially entirely `_FULL_CELL_MILLI`-dominated** — only
0.60% of the reported cost reflects genuine geometric/purpose/congestion routing
decisions; 99.4% is the fixed penalty for stepping into an already-full or
foreign-plane cell. 125 such steps occur across the 39 connections' winning paths.
This is the same mechanism diagnosed for the runtime (§1): A* is spending its
search budget pricing (and, per the heuristic, barely discouraging) moves into
cells the router will never actually choose to sit in, because the octile
heuristic (~1,000 milli/step) is five orders of magnitude smaller than the
penalty it is trying to route around.

### 6b. Bundle shared-corridor double counting

Confirmed and quantified precisely (correcting the "7 SPI members -> 7x" estimate
in NETCLASS_PLAN.md — the actual multiplicity varies per bundle, not a flat 7x):

| Bundle | Member connections that reuse its corridor cost | Corridor cost (milli, counted once) | Contribution to total as summed (cost x reuse count) |
|---|---|---|---|
| SPI:U4->U7 | 4 (CLK, CS0, MISO, MOSI) | 10,203,499 | 40,813,996 |
| I2C:U4->U5 | 2 (SCL, SDA) | 5,095,383 | 10,190,766 |
| SPI:U4->U8 | 1 (CS1 only — CLK/MISO/MOSI already claimed by U7, first in the bundle-id sort order) | 10,396,382 | 10,396,382 |
| SPI:U4->U9 | 1 (CS2 only, same reason) | 10,145,802 | 10,145,802 |
| **Sum (as reported in `total_est_cost_milli`)** | 8 connections | — | **71,546,946** |
| **Sum if counted once per bundle (4 bundles)** | — | — | **35,841,066** |
| **Inflation** | — | — | **35,705,880 (5.68% of the 628,753,921 total)** |

Root cause, confirmed against the code: `net_to_bundle` maps each net to the
**first** bundle (by sorted `id`) that lists it as a member, via `setdefault` —
since `I2C:U4->U5` < `SPI:U4->U7` < `SPI:U4->U8` < `SPI:U4->U9` alphabetically, and
CLK/MISO/MOSI are members of all three SPI bundles' `member_nets`, they get
permanently claimed by `SPI:U4->U7`; only each destination's own dedicated
chip-select net (CS0/CS1/CS2) plus the first bundle's shared trio actually route
independently. `global_route`'s per-connection loop then reports (and sums into
`total_est_cost_milli`) the **same** `est_cost_milli` once per net that maps to a
given bundle — not once per bundle — which is the inflation above. Recommend the
eventual MCP tool docs state this precisely (member-count-dependent, not a flat
multiplier) rather than the placeholder "7x" figure.

## 7. Direction-inference dominance fractions

`infer_layer_directions(kiln)` — length-weighted H/V histogram, `_DOMINANCE_FRAC =
0.60`, `_MIN_INFER_LEN_MM = 10.0`, `_DIAG_LO/_HI = 30/60 deg`:

| Layer | Type | h_len_mm | v_len_mm | Total classified mm | V fraction | H fraction | Resolved direction | Source |
|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|---|
| F.Cu | signal | 859.3098 | 960.1141 | 1,819.4239 | **52.77%** | 47.23% | **None** | inferred (under 60% threshold) |
| B.Cu | signal | 247.5354 | 296.7789 | 544.3143 | **54.52%** | 45.48% | **None** | inferred (under 60% threshold) |
| In1.Cu | power | 0.0 | 0.0 | 0.0 | — | — | None | power (rule 2: planes never get a direction, regardless of copper) |
| In2.Cu | power | 0.0 | 0.0 | 0.0 | — | — | None | power (rule 2) |

**Correction to the task's illustrative example:** the task's prompt suggested
checking "F.Cu is 54% H"; the actual measured direction is the *opposite* — **F.Cu
is 52.77% V-leaning** (not H), and B.Cu is 54.52% V-leaning. Both signal layers
lean vertical, neither reaches the 60% dominance bar, so both correctly resolve to
`None` per the current threshold — this is not a bug, it is the documented
behavior of an unmet dominance bar. `In1.Cu`/`In2.Cu` are the two power-type
layers; they have **zero** classified segment length (planes have no discrete
routed segments to classify) and get `None` from rule 2 regardless of any
threshold — tuning the dominance fraction cannot change their outcome.

**Threshold recommendation:** both signal layers sit in a narrow 52.8–54.5% V
band — a genuinely weak majority, not a clear two-layer H/V grid design (kiln's
F.Cu/B.Cu carry a fairly even routing mix, consistent with hand-routed traces
following component geometry rather than a systematic X/Y layer split). Numerically:

- Lowering the threshold to **53%** resolves `B.Cu` to `"v"` (54.52% > 53%) but
  leaves `F.Cu` at `None` (52.77% < 53%) — inconsistent treatment of two layers
  whose fractions differ by less than 2 points.
- Lowering the threshold to **52%** resolves **both** `F.Cu` and `B.Cu` to `"v"`.
- The current 60% threshold treats both as `None`, which then falls to
  `_MIN_INFER_LEN_MM`'s alternation path only if a layer's classified length is
  under 10 mm — not the case here (1,819 mm and 544 mm respectively) — so on
  kiln, `None` is the **final** answer for F.Cu/B.Cu today, not a fallback that
  gets fixed up later; every 7.3c off-direction cost term (`_direction_factor`)
  is therefore a no-op on this board (`layer_dir not in ("h","v")` short-circuits
  to neutral 1.0 for every planar move).

Recommendation for the coordinator: **do not blindly lower the threshold to 52%**
just to force a call on this one board — a 52.8%/54.5% majority is closer to noise
than to a genuine routing-grid signal, and forcing `"v"` would apply a real
`off_direction` cost penalty (default 2.0x) to every horizontal move on both
copper layers for comparatively weak evidence. If a lower threshold is wanted,
53% is the more defensible number (it only accepts B.Cu's genuinely larger
1.9-point margin over 50/50, leaving F.Cu's near-coin-flip at `None`) — but the
cleaner fix is probably not a global threshold change at all: consider a
per-layer confidence band (e.g. only infer when the majority exceeds 60% **or**
the H/V length gap exceeds some absolute mm margin) so a board like kiln, with no
real per-layer routing-axis convention, correctly stays `None` on both layers
rather than being pushed one way by a coin-flip-adjacent majority.
